Objetivos

El grupo de investigación en Patología Oncológica y Bioinformática es una plataforma específica para actividades de investigación directamente relacionadas con dos objetivos principales:

  • Identificar patrones de respuesta inmune i factores etiológicos, fisiopatogénicos y modelos prognósticos relacionados con la progresión de neoplasias.
  • Desenvolupar y perfeccionar nuevos soportes informáticos de análisis de imagen asistidos por ordenador para permitir una cuantificación automatizada más precisa i reproducible de estos marcadores celulares.

El grado de implicación de estos patrones en los mecanismos de escape tumoral y en la utilidad terapéutica es avalua al mismo tiempo que la descripcón de patrones de los virus oncogénicos implicados en el desarrollo de esos neoplasias.

Estos objetivos pueden ayudar a entender los mecanismos biológicos implicados en la resistencia a la quimioterapia y en la obtención de nuevas dianas terapéuticas capaces de intervención, además de la constitución de nuevos factores clave en el diagnóstico, pronóstico y tratamiento de estos pacientes.

Lineas de investigación

  • Estudio de la celularidad limfoide reactiva y la respuesta inmune en síndromes limfoproliferativos. Estudio de las implicaciones pronósticas y los mecanismos de escape tumoral.
  • Potencial oncogénico de virus en linfomas: análisis de la expresión celular de virus con potencial oncogénico en biopsias de linfomas y su correlación serológica.
  • Morfometria e informatica aplicada: desarrollo de patrones de análisis de imágenes digitales assistido por ordenador para avaluar cuantitativamente de manera automatizada marcadores específicos de subpoblaciones celulares tumorales y de respuesta inmune con tinciones inmunohistoquímicas i de fluorescencia.
  • Avaluación de patrones de marcadores celulares oncológicos, de respuesa inmune y de supervivencia den cáncer de mama.

Dr. Carlos López

Is the coordinator of the research group PO&B. He graduated B.Sc. in Biology from the Autonomous University of Barcelona in 2001 and received his PhD from the Rovira I Virgili University of Tarragona in 2010. He is actively involved in a number of projects of investigation. Current area of research is on the elaboration of specific patterns of computer-assisted image analysis (algorithms) for quantitative automatic evaluation of immunohistochemical molecular target.

Dra. Marylène Lejeune

Graduated B.Sc. in Medical Biology in 1990 at the Free University of Brussels in Belgium and received his Ph.D. from the same University in 1995. She participated in diverse projects of investigation centred on neutrophils functionality in cancer patients (Belgium) and therapeutic vaccine with dendritic cells in AIDS patients (Spain). She is currently a full-time Clinical Investigator at the Hospital de Tortosa Verge de la Cinta. Current areas of research include the study of immune response in human cancers by means of immunohistochemical techniques, digital image analysis and fluorescent in situ hybridization.

Dr. Ramón Bosch

Is currently head of the Department of Pathology of the Hospital de Tortosa Verge de la Cinta, Spain. He is a full-time Medical Pathologist at the Hospital de Tortosa Verge de la Cinta, Spain. He graduated in Medicine at the Rovira and Virgili University of Reus in 1990 and then specialized in Pathology in 1994 at the Vall d’Hebron University Hospital, Spain. His main interests are the study of etiopathogenic relationships between virus and lymphoid neoplasms as well as the cellular pattern of lymphoid responses.

Dr. Tomás Álvaro

Received his Medical Pathologist degree from the Complutense University of Madrid, Spain, in 1985 and obtained his Ph.D. from the University of Granada in 1989. He is actively involved in a number of projects of investigation and is a manuscript reviewer for different international journals. He had authored a dozen manuscripts in the main field of Medical Oncology. His works concern principally the study of immune response in Hodgkin’s lymphomas and different lymphoproliferative syndromes. Current areas of research interest include the study of changes of genes and proteins involved in the host immune response but also in the apoptotic/proliferative pathways of tumoural cells in Hodgkin lymphomas.

Dr. Lluís E. Pons

Graduated in Medicine at the University of Barcelona 1998 and then specialized in Pathology in 2003 in the Parc Taulí Sanitary Corporation of Sabadell, Spain. He joined the staff of the Department of Pathology at Hospital de Tortosa Verge de la Cinta in 2003. At present he works on the human papilloma virus (HPV) cervical infection and more particularly on the clinicopathological evaluation of immune response pathways and tumour escape mechanisms in carcinoma of uterine neck and cervical preneoplasic lesions.

Dr. Joaquín Jaén

Graduated in Medicine at the Literary University ofValencia in 1988 and then specialized in Pathology in the University Hospital La Fe of Valencia in 1999. At present, he is a full-time Medical Pathologist at the Hospital de Tortosa Verge de la Cinta, Spain. His main interests are the development of computerized analysis algorithms for automatic digital images quantification of immunohistochemical techniques.

Dra. Maria-Teresa Salvadó

She is a full-time Cytotechnologist at the Hospital de TortosaVerge de la Cinta, Spain and professor associate in the Nurse School of Rovira and Virgil University. She cursed a degree in “Methodology of Investigation: Design and Statistics in Sciences of the Health” at the Autonomous University of Barcelona in 2007. She continuously collaborates with all the investigators of the Hospital providing statistical evaluation and support to the clinical studies.
